## What to Look for in a Home NVR

Before diving into picks, here's what actually matters when you're comparing NVRs for home use:

### Channels

How many cameras can it record simultaneously? Start by counting how many cameras you have *now*, then think about where you might add more. Leave headroom — it's far cheaper to buy a 16-channel NVR upfront than to replace an 8-channel in two years.

**General guidance:**
- 1–3 bed house, 4 cameras → 8-channel NVR is fine
- Larger property, side/rear/front + driveway → 16-channel
- Full property coverage with indoor and outbuildings → 36-channel

### PoE Built-in vs External Switch

Some NVRs have built-in PoE ports — you plug cameras directly into the NVR. Others (like the Reolink RLN36) have no PoE, so cameras connect to a separate PoE switch which connects to the NVR via uplink.

**Built-in PoE** is simpler and cheaper for small setups. **External PoE switch** gives more flexibility — you can place cameras further from the NVR, add more cameras beyond what the NVR's PoE supports, and use a dedicated switch with a higher power budget.

### Storage

Most home NVRs take 1–2 standard 3.5" HDDs. NVR-grade drives (WD Red, Seagate IronWolf) are designed for 24/7 operation and are worth the premium. At 4K continuous recording:

- 1TB → approx. 3–4 days of footage per camera
- 4TB → approx. 12–16 days per camera
- 8TB → approx. 3–4 weeks per camera

For my RLN36 with five cameras, I installed three WD Red 4TB drives. Comfortable headroom for 24/7 recording with motion-trigger alerts.

### ONVIF and Home Assistant

ONVIF is a standard protocol for IP cameras and NVRs. If your NVR and cameras are ONVIF-compatible, you can mix brands freely. It also means you can integrate your NVR into [Home Assistant](/posts/home-assistant-guide/) for motion alerts, snapshots, and live feeds on dashboards.

Reolink NVRs support ONVIF and integrate well with Home Assistant via the official Reolink integration.

## NVR vs Software (Frigate / Blue Iris)

Worth mentioning: a dedicated NVR isn't the only option. **Frigate** is an open-source NVR that runs on your own hardware (a Raspberry Pi 5, an old PC, or a Home Assistant box with a Coral TPU) and integrates natively with Home Assistant. **Blue Iris** is a Windows-only alternative with a one-time licence fee.

**Choose a dedicated NVR if:**
- You want plug-and-play reliability
- You don't want to maintain software
- You're not running a home server already

**Choose Frigate/Blue Iris if:**
- You already have a home server
- You want tight Home Assistant integration
- You want AI person/vehicle detection without a subscription

For most people, a dedicated NVR is the right call. For those already deep in Home Assistant, Frigate is worth the setup effort.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is an NVR and how does it differ from a DVR?

An NVR (Network Video Recorder) records from IP cameras over a network (Ethernet or WiFi). A DVR (Digital Video Recorder) records from analogue cameras using coaxial cable. NVRs offer higher resolution, easier installation, and ONVIF compatibility. Almost all modern home CCTV systems use NVRs.

### Do I need an NVR if I have a Reolink camera?

Not necessarily — Reolink cameras can record locally to an SD card and you can view them via the Reolink app without an NVR. But an NVR gives you centralised storage, easy multi-camera review, and longer retention periods. For more than two or three cameras, an NVR is strongly recommended.

### Can I use any cameras with any NVR?

If both the NVR and cameras support ONVIF, you can generally mix brands. In practice, you'll get the best feature support (motion zones, smart detection, bi-directional audio) by staying within the same brand ecosystem — Reolink cameras with a Reolink NVR, for example.

### How much storage do I need?

A rough guide: 1TB per camera per week of 24/7 4K recording. Motion-only recording dramatically reduces storage — a camera that's only triggering a few times a day might only use 50–100GB per week. For most homes with 4–6 cameras on motion recording, 4–8TB total is comfortable.

### Does the NVR need an internet connection?

No — NVRs record locally to hard drives and work entirely without internet. You'll want internet connectivity for remote viewing via a mobile app, but recordings are always stored on-device. This is one of the key advantages over cloud-based camera systems.

### Can I connect my NVR to Home Assistant?

Yes, if it supports ONVIF or has a native Home Assistant integration. Reolink NVRs work with the official Reolink integration in Home Assistant, giving you motion triggers, snapshots, and camera feeds in your dashboards. See our [Home Assistant guide](/posts/home-assistant-guide/) for more.

### What hard drives should I use in an NVR?

Use NVR/NAS-rated drives designed for 24/7 operation. The main options are **WD Red** and **Seagate IronWolf**. Avoid desktop drives (WD Blue, Seagate Barracuda) — they're not designed for always-on workloads and will fail faster.
