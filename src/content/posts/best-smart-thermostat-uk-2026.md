# Best Smart Thermostat UK 2026

*Last updated: May 2026 | Tested and researched for UK buyers*

---

Heating is typically the biggest energy cost in a UK home — accounting for around 60% of your annual energy bill. A smart thermostat won't perform miracles, but the right one can trim 15–25% off your heating costs while making your home genuinely more comfortable.

I've been running smart home setups for years and have tested heating integrations across multiple platforms. Here's the honest breakdown UK buyers need before spending £100–£250 on a thermostat.

## 1. Google Nest Learning Thermostat — Best Overall

**Price:** ~£219 (thermostat only) / ~£249 with Pro installation  
**Monthly fee:** None  
**Compatibility:** Google Home, Alexa, Matter  

The Nest Learning Thermostat is the gold standard for a reason. It learns your schedule over the first week of use, starts anticipating when you want heat, and has the most polished app of any smart thermostat on the market.

The premium price is real — but so is the product quality. The display is beautiful, the hardware feels premium, and the energy-saving features genuinely work. Nest's "Home/Away Assist" uses your phone's location to automatically drop the temperature when you leave, and the Nest app gives you genuinely useful monthly energy reports that help identify waste.

### Why It's the Top Pick

- **Auto-schedule** — learns your habits and programs itself
- **Home/Away Assist** — saves energy automatically when you're out
- **Energy History** — full breakdown of what drove your usage
- **Matter support** — works with Home Assistant and future-proof
- **Farsight display** — shows time, temperature, or weather when you walk past

### Cons

- Expensive upfront (~£220 just for the thermostat)
- Requires a common wire (C-wire) in some older UK systems — check before buying
- Google account required — some privacy trade-off

### Integration with Home Assistant

Nest integrates with [Home Assistant](/posts/home-assistant-guide/) via the Nest integration or via Matter. If you're already running a smart home hub, this is the most capable option for deep automation (combining heating with motion sensors, presence detection, etc.). See our [complete Home Assistant setup guide](/posts/home-assistant-guide/) for more.

## 2. Hive Active Heating — Best for British Gas Customers

**Price:** ~£179 (starter kit with hub)  
**Monthly fee:** None  
**Compatibility:** Amazon Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit  

Hive is the UK-native option and it shows. The app is tailored for British boilers, the support is UK-based, and if you're a British Gas customer you can often get discounted installation deals. The Hive Hub connects via your router and manages both heating and hot water independently.

The recent Hive app refresh made it significantly better. Scheduling is intuitive, geofencing works reliably, and the multi-zone support (if your boiler supports it) is excellent for larger homes.

### Why Choose Hive

- **UK-first design** — built around British boiler systems
- **No monthly fee** — unlike tado°'s premium features
- **Strong ecosystem** — Hive also does smart plugs, bulbs, sensors, and security cameras
- **Hot water control** — separate smart control for your hot water tank
- **British Gas deals** — installation discounts frequently available

### Cons

- Requires the Hive Hub (extra hardware)
- App quality still slightly behind Nest
- Less smart learning behaviour vs Nest — more manual scheduling

## 3. tado° Smart Thermostat V3+ — Best for Energy Savings

**Price:** ~£149 (starter kit)  
**Monthly fee:** Free / £2.99/month for "Auto-Assist" premium features  
**Compatibility:** Amazon Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit  

tado° is the energy nerd's choice. The free tier is genuinely good, but the "Auto-Assist" subscription unlocks geofencing automation and the most detailed energy analytics of any thermostat on this list. If you want to obsess over your heating data and extract maximum efficiency, tado° wins.

The app shows exactly what drove your heating on any given day — outside temperature, how long the boiler ran, when windows were open (tado° detects this via temperature drops) — and calculates your actual energy savings over time.

### Why Choose tado°

- **Best energy analytics** — bar none
- **Open Window Detection** — automatically pauses heating if a window is opened
- **Multi-zone support** — smart radiator valves (TRVs) available for room-by-room control
- **HomeKit integration** — best Apple Home support of the bunch
- **Self-install** — typically a straightforward DIY job

### Cons

- Auto-Assist geofencing requires a £2.99/month subscription
- Less "premium feel" hardware compared to Nest
- Initial setup can be fiddly on older boilers

### Room-by-Room Control with tado° TRVs

tado° stands out because you can add Smart Radiator Thermostats (TRVs) to individual radiators, giving you room-by-room control. This pairs brilliantly with [Zigbee sensors](/posts/best-zigbee-sensors-uk-2025/) if you're building a full smart home — trigger heating only in occupied rooms.

## 4. Drayton Wiser — Best Budget Option

**Price:** ~£129 (starter kit)  
**Monthly fee:** None  
**Compatibility:** Amazon Alexa, Google Home  

Drayton Wiser is the underdog that punches above its price. Made by Drayton (a trusted UK heating brand since 1938), it offers multi-zone control, TRV support, and a solid app at a significantly lower price than the competition.

The trade-off is polish. The app is functional but not beautiful, the learning features are minimal, and it lacks Apple HomeKit support. But if you want reliable smart heating without subscriptions or premium prices, Wiser is excellent value.

### Why Choose Wiser

- **No subscription fees ever**
- **Multi-zone out of the box** — cheaper than tado° for multi-room setups
- **UK brand** — designed for UK systems, good customer support
- **TRV support** — room-by-room control available

### Cons

- No Apple HomeKit support
- App not as polished as Nest or tado°
- Fewer smart learning features

## Do Smart Thermostats Actually Save Money?

Short answer: yes, but not as much as marketing suggests.

Real-world savings for UK homes typically land between **£80–£150/year** depending on:
- How inefficient your current setup is (manual thermostats waste more)
- How actively you use the scheduling and geofencing features
- Your home's insulation and heating system efficiency

At £149–£219 upfront, you're looking at a **1–2 year payback period** in most cases. After that, it's pure saving.

The biggest gains come from **geofencing** (auto-off when you leave) and **schedules** (not heating an empty house during work hours). Both features are available free on every thermostat in this list.

## Compatibility Check: Will It Work With My Boiler?

All four thermostats on this list work with **most UK combi boilers** (which cover ~70% of UK homes). Check the following:

1. **Combi boiler** — all compatible, straightforward installation
2. **System boiler with hot water cylinder** — check Hive or tado° (both support separate HW control)
3. **OpenTherm compatibility** — tado° and Nest support OpenTherm for more efficient modulation (check your boiler manual)
4. **Old back boiler systems** — may need an adapter; contact the manufacturer first

## FAQ

### What is the best smart thermostat for Home Assistant?

Google Nest Learning Thermostat integrates best with [Home Assistant](/posts/home-assistant-guide/) via the native Nest integration or Matter. tado° also has a community integration. Both allow deep automation — combining presence detection, temperature sensors, and custom schedules.

### Do I need a subscription for smart thermostat features?

Most core features are free. tado° charges £2.99/month for Auto-Assist (geofencing automation). All scheduling, manual control, and basic energy reports are free across all options.

### Can I install a smart thermostat myself?

Yes for most — tado°, Drayton Wiser, and Google Nest all support DIY installation. Hive recommends professional installation but it's doable DIY if you're comfortable with basic wiring. Always turn off your boiler at the mains before touching any wiring.

### Will a smart thermostat work with my system boiler and hot water tank?

Yes — Hive Active Heating and tado° both support separate control of heating and hot water. Google Nest requires the Nest Heat Link (included in full kits) which also supports hot water control on compatible systems.

### What's the difference between a smart thermostat and smart radiator valves (TRVs)?

A smart thermostat controls your boiler — it tells it when to turn on and off. Smart TRVs (available from tado°, Drayton Wiser, Hive) give you room-by-room temperature control by regulating individual radiators. For maximum efficiency, combine both: a smart thermostat to control the boiler and TRVs to balance individual rooms.

### Do smart thermostats work with heat pumps?

Google Nest Learning Thermostat 4th Gen has specific heat pump support. tado° also supports heat pumps via their dedicated kit. As heat pumps become more common in the UK, this is worth checking — confirm compatibility before purchasing.
