## Why Your Doorbell Choice Matters More Than You Think

Most people buy on price. But the real cost of a video doorbell isn't the upfront hardware — it's the **ongoing subscription**.

Ring charges £3.49/month (£41.88/year) for cloud storage. Over 5 years, that's over £200 *on top* of the hardware cost. Nest Aware is £5/month. Arlo's free tier is three clips per day. These add up.

The smarter play for most UK buyers: choose a doorbell with **local storage** or **no subscription required**, especially if you already have a NAS, NVR, or Home Assistant set up at home.

## 1. Eufy Video Doorbell E340 — Best Overall

**Best for:** Most UK buyers who want quality without monthly fees

The E340 is the standout pick in 2026. Unlike Ring, Eufy stores footage **locally** on its HomeBase hub — no monthly subscription required for basic recording. The dual-lens design (wide-angle for faces, package-detection lens pointed down) is genuinely clever and works well in practice.

**What we like:**
- 2K resolution with HDR — excellent face detail in daylight and dusk
- Dual-lens: 160° wide + 90° downward package detection  
- HomeBase local storage — no mandatory cloud subscription  
- Human and vehicle detection, not just motion blobs  
- Works with Alexa and Google Home  
- Optional HomeBase connectivity for Home Assistant via local API

**What we don't:**
- HomeBase required for local storage (extra box)
- App can be slow to load live view
- Night colour vision not as good as Reolink in full dark

**Verdict:** If you want the best balance of features, video quality, and no subscription, the E340 wins comfortably.

## 2. Reolink Video Doorbell PoE — Best for Home Assistant / NVR Users

**Best for:** Anyone already running Reolink cameras or a Home Assistant/NVR setup

If you've already got a Reolink NVR or a [PoE camera system](/posts/best-poe-security-cameras-uk-2025/), the Reolink doorbell is the obvious choice. It runs on standard PoE, records directly to your NVR or NAS, and streams via RTSP — meaning full Home Assistant integration with zero cloud dependency.

**What we like:**

**Verdict:** Fine product, but the subscription tax makes it expensive long-term. Only choose Ring if you're fully in the Amazon/Alexa ecosystem.

**Verdict:** Great hardware but expensive to run. Best in a Google household that's already paying for Nest Aware.

## Wired vs Battery: Which Should You Choose?

**Wired (hardwired or PoE)**
- Always on — never runs flat  
- Continuous recording possible  
- More reliable motion detection  
- Requires existing bell wiring or running cable  

**Battery**
- No wiring needed — perfect for renters  
- Easy install anywhere  
- Requires recharging every 1-6 months  
- Can't do continuous recording (drains battery too fast)  

**Our recommendation:** Wired if you own your home and can run cable. Battery if you're renting or don't want the hassle. The Reolink PoE option is the gold standard for wired.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do video doorbells work in the UK?

Yes — all the doorbells listed here are sold and supported in the UK. Ring, Eufy, Google Nest, and Arlo all have UK-specific support and use standard UK electrical standards (8-24V doorbell transformers).

### Can I use a video doorbell without a subscription?

**Yes** — but only with Eufy (local storage) or Reolink PoE (NVR/NAS storage). Ring, Nest, and Arlo all require paid subscriptions for meaningful video history.

### Is Reolink worth it?

If you care about privacy, local storage, and Home Assistant integration: **absolutely**. The Reolink PoE doorbell pairs perfectly with their [NVR](/posts/reolink-rln36-nvr-review/) and [fisheye camera](/posts/reolink-fisheye-camera-review/) for a complete home security system with no cloud dependency.

### Do video doorbells need WiFi?

Most do. The exception is **Reolink PoE**, which only needs an Ethernet cable — data and power over one cable, no WiFi required. This is why it's the most reliable option.

### What's the difference between Ring and Eufy?

Ring: Excellent app and ecosystem, but requires ongoing subscription fees. Eufy: Good app, local storage option, no subscription required for basic functionality. Eufy wins on value; Ring wins if you're already deep in the Amazon ecosystem.

### Do I need existing doorbell wiring?

**Battery doorbells** (Ring 4, Eufy E340 battery option) can install without any wiring. **Wired doorbells** (Reolink PoE, Nest Wired) require either a doorbell transformer or PoE infrastructure. Reolink needs a PoE switch like the [Gigaplus 2.5G](/posts/gigaplus-2-5g-poe-switch-10g-sfp-review/) or [Yuanley 18-port](/posts/yuanley-18-port-poe-switch-review/).
