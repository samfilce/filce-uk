## The Best Smart Doorbells UK 2026

### 1. Eufy Video Doorbell E340 — Best Overall (No Subscription)

**Best for:** Homeowners who want great features without a monthly fee

The Eufy E340 is the doorbell we'd recommend to most people. Dual-lens design gives you a wide 160° horizontal view *and* a separate downward-facing lens so you can actually see parcels left on the step — not just the person's face. 2K resolution is sharp, colour night vision is genuinely useful, and everything stores locally on the Eufy HomeBase.

**No subscription required.** That alone sets it apart from Ring and Arlo.

For Home Assistant users, Eufy supports RTSP streams and has decent community integration. It's not perfect, but it works.

**Pros:**
- Dual-lens catches parcels on doorstep
- Local storage via HomeBase — no cloud fees
- 2K resolution with colour night vision
- Works wired (existing doorbell wiring) or battery

**Cons:**
- HomeBase adds bulk and cost
- Eufy's cloud privacy has had historical controversy (now resolved)
- App is functional but not slick

**Verdict:** The best value smart doorbell in the UK right now. Especially compelling if you're already on Eufy's ecosystem or hate subscriptions.

### 2. Ring Video Doorbell 4 — Best Ecosystem

**Best for:** People already in the Amazon/Ring ecosystem

Ring is the household name, and version 4 is a solid product. Pre-roll video capture (4 seconds of footage before motion is detected) is genuinely useful. The two-way audio is clear, setup is dead simple, and Alexa integration is seamless if you're an Echo household.

The catch: **Ring Protect subscription** is £3.49/month (Basic) or £8/month (Plus) to actually access recorded footage. Without it, you get live view only — no history, no package alerts, no rich notifications.

UK wiring note: Ring 4 works with UK 8–24V AC doorbell wiring, or battery.

**Pros:**
- Massive ecosystem — works with Ring Alarm, Floodlights, etc.
- Dead-simple setup and app
- Alexa integration is best-in-class
- Battery or wired

**Cons:**
- Subscription essentially required for useful features
- 1080p in 2026 feels behind competitors
- No local storage option

**Verdict:** Great if you're already in the Ring/Amazon ecosystem and don't mind the sub. If you're starting fresh, Eufy offers more for less.

### 3. Google Nest Doorbell (Wired, 2nd Gen) — Best for Google Home

**Best for:** Google Home users, or anyone who wants the slickest UX

Google's wired Nest Doorbell is the premium pick. 960p HDR might sound lower-res on paper, but the HDR processing is excellent — it handles backlit doorways (bright sky, dark face) better than most competitors. Three hours of event history are free without a subscription, which is genuinely usable.

The "Familiar Faces" feature (recognises regular visitors) requires Nest Aware at £6/month. But the core experience without it is still strong.

Requires existing wired doorbell installation (8–24V AC). No battery option for the wired gen.

**Pros:**
- Best HDR/dynamic range of any doorbell tested
- Slickest app and notification UX
- 3 hours free event history (no sub needed)
- Google Assistant integration

**Cons:**
- Wired only — needs existing doorbell circuit
- Familiar Faces and longer history need Nest Aware subscription
- 960p vs 2K competitors feels like a step back

**Verdict:** Best smart doorbell if you're a Google Home household and have wired doorbell wiring already in place.

## Wired vs Battery: Which Should You Choose?

**Go wired if:** You have existing UK doorbell wiring (8–24V AC), you want 24/7 reliability, and you don't want to think about charging. Most UK homes built after ~1990 have wired doorbell circuits.

**Go battery if:** You're renting, have no existing wiring, or need a quick install. Just accept you'll need to charge every 1–6 months depending on activity level.

**Go PoE if:** You're a home automation enthusiast running a NAS, NVR, or Home Assistant. One cable does everything.

## FAQ

### Do smart doorbells work with UK wiring?

Yes — most wired smart doorbells work with standard UK 8–24V AC doorbell circuits. Ring, Eufy, Google Nest, and Arlo all support this. If you're unsure, check your existing transformer voltage before buying. Most PoE doorbells (like Reolink) bypass this entirely and just need a Cat5e/Cat6 cable.

### Is Ring worth it in the UK?

Ring makes solid hardware, but the subscription model is a significant ongoing cost. If you want full functionality without monthly fees, Eufy is the better choice for most UK buyers.

### Which smart doorbell works without a subscription?

**Eufy** and **Reolink** are the standout options. Eufy stores footage locally on the HomeBase; Reolink stores to your own NVR or NAS. Both offer full feature sets with zero monthly cost.

### Can I use a smart doorbell with Home Assistant?

Yes. Reolink's PoE Doorbell is the best option — it streams natively over RTSP and has an official Home Assistant integration. Eufy also works well with RTSP enabled. See our [Home Assistant guide](/posts/home-assistant-guide/) for setup.

### What's the best smart doorbell for a rented property?

Battery-powered options like the Ring Video Doorbell 4 or Eufy E340 (battery version) are the easiest installs with no wiring required. Just note Ring needs a subscription for recorded footage.

### How do smart doorbells handle the UK's dark winters?

Look for colour night vision (Eufy E340 has this) or HDR processing (Google Nest). Standard IR night vision gives black-and-white footage — fine for identification, but colour is more useful for distinguishing clothing or vehicle colour.
