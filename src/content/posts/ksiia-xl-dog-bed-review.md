**Rating: ⭐⭐ (2/5)**

## Short Version

It lost its shape after the first wash. We replaced it quickly. Not recommended for large dogs that need regular washing.

---

## What Happened

Out of the box it looked the part — large, plush, the kind of calming bed that's supposed to help anxious or restless dogs settle. For Ice (our Alaskan Malamute) we needed XL, and this fit the bill on paper.

First wash: the filling shifted, the bolster sides collapsed, and it never recovered its original shape. What had been a structured bed became a flat, uneven mat. Not what you want when you've bought it specifically for the raised sides and the calming effect those provide.

---

## The Problem with Cheap Calming Beds

This isn't unique to KSIIA — it's a common issue with budget calming dog beds. The fluffy filling that makes them look appealing in the listing isn't always resilient enough to survive regular washing, especially at the sizes large dogs need. The bolsters flatten, the shape goes, and you're left with something that costs nearly as much as a better bed would have.

For a small dog that rarely needs washing, this might hold up fine. For a large dog that gets muddy, sheds heavily, or simply needs regular cleaning, you need something built tougher.

---

## Verdict

Didn't last past the first wash. For the price there are better options — look for beds with removable, separately washable covers and denser filling rather than loose polyfill. We moved on quickly and won't be going back to this one.
