## Why UK Smart Locks Are Different

Before diving into picks, a quick primer on why you can't just buy whatever Wirecutter recommends:

**UK door types:**
- **Mortise locks** — the mechanism sits inside the door body. Very common in UK homes. Need a replacement cylinder (euro cylinder) smart lock.
- **Yale nightlatch** — the spring-loaded latch on the inside of the door. Common on older UK front doors as a secondary lock.
- **Multi-point locks** — one handle engages three or more locking points. Common on UPVC doors. Most smart locks **don't work** with these.

**Insurance compliance:** Your home insurance may require a BS3621 mortise deadlock. A smart add-on alone won't satisfy this — check your policy.

## Installation: What You Need to Know

**Before buying, check:**

1. **Door thickness:** Most smart locks support 35-55mm. Thicker doors may need adapters.
2. **Cylinder type:** Euro cylinder? Rim cylinder? Thumb turn on inside? Take a photo of your door and check the manufacturer's compatibility checker.
3. **Power nearby:** Some locks (like Yale Conexis with WiFi bridge) need power near the door for the bridge.
4. **Insurance:** Call your insurer. Some require a mortise deadlock for contents cover.

**Installation difficulty:**
- **Thumb-turn add-ons (Nuki, Tedee):** 10 minutes. Peel, stick, done.
- **Replacement cylinders (Yale Conexis, Ultion):** 30-60 minutes. Requires removing old cylinder, fitting new one, aligning cam.

## FAQ

### Do smart locks work with Home Assistant?
Yes — but integration quality varies. Nuki and Tedee have **native** Home Assistant integrations. Yale uses cloud APIs which are less reliable and slower.

### Will a smart lock invalidate my home insurance?
Possibly, if you remove your only BS3621 lock. Check your policy. The Yale Conexis + BS rim cylinder and Ultion SMART both meet British Standards. Nuki/Tedee are add-ons — your original lock stays, so compliance depends on that.

### What happens if the battery dies?
- **Nuki:** Emergency unlock via 9V battery on the outside contacts, or physical key through the original lock.
- **Yale Conexis:** Physical key through the rim cylinder (if fitted), or 9V emergency power.
- **Tedee:** No physical key — keep spare batteries handy or use the app via Bridge if you have one.

### Can I use a smart lock on a multi-point UPVC door?
Generally **no**. Smart locks are designed for single-point locks. Multi-point systems require motorised handles or specialist UPVC smart locks (very rare in UK).

### What's the difference between Matter and Zigbee?
Matter is the new universal smart home standard (Apple, Google, Amazon, Samsung all support it). Zigbee is the older mesh network standard. Most smart locks use Bluetooth + Matter/WiFi, not Zigbee directly. But they connect to Home Assistant, which then controls your Zigbee devices.
