## Zigbee vs WiFi vs Matter — Which Protocol Should You Choose?

This is the most important decision before buying any smart bulbs. Here's the short version:

**Choose Zigbee if:**
- You're already running Home Assistant with a [Zigbee coordinator](/posts/sonoff-poe-review/)
- You want low-latency, local-first automations
- You're building a large installation (Zigbee mesh improves with more devices)
- Battery life and mesh reliability matter

**Choose WiFi if:**
- You want the simplest setup (app on phone, no hub)
- You're only buying a few bulbs
- You don't want to invest in Zigbee infrastructure

**Choose Matter if:**
- You want ecosystem flexibility (works with everything)
- You're future-proofing your smart home
- You want local control without vendor lock-in

## Essential Smart Bulb Automations

Here are the automations I actually run daily with smart bulbs:

**Sunrise Alarm:** Gradual brightness from 1% to 100% over 30 minutes before wake-up time. Colour temperature shifts from warm red (2200K) through daylight white (4000K).

**Motion Night Light:** Low brightness (10%) warm white motion-triggered lighting for hallways and bathrooms after 10pm. Stays on for 3 minutes after motion stops.

**Evening Dim:** All living areas fade to 40% brightness and 2700K warm white at sunset. Signals "evening mode" to the whole household.

**Leave Home:** One button sets every bulb to off. For Zigbee bulbs this works even if the internet is down.

## FAQ

### What is the difference between warm white and cool white?

Warm white (2700K) is yellowish, like traditional incandescent bulbs — comfortable for living spaces. Cool white (4000K+) is bluish — better for task lighting and daylight simulation. Smart bulbs can switch between both.

### Do I need a Philips Hue Bridge for Zigbee bulbs?

No. Any Zigbee coordinator works — including the [SONOFF Zigbee/Thread PoE Controller](/posts/sonoff-poe-review/), ConBee II, or SkyConnect. Hue bulbs work perfectly via Home Assistant without the official Hue Bridge.

### What is Matter and why does it matter?

Matter is a new smart home standard backed by Apple, Google, Amazon, and Samsung. It means a Matter-certified bulb will work with Home Assistant, HomeKit, Alexa, and Google Home without proprietary hubs. It also enables local control.

### Are smart bulbs expensive to run?

No — most LED smart bulbs use 6-10 watts at full brightness. A smart bulb running 3 hours daily costs roughly £1-2 per year in electricity. The convenience is easily worth the energy cost.

### Do smart bulbs work when the internet is down?

With Home Assistant and a Zigbee coordinator, yes — automations and manual control work locally. WiFi-only bulbs may depend on cloud services unless specifically configured for local control (LIFX, Matter bulbs).
