## FAQ

## Which smart plug is best for UK homes in 2025?

The **TP-Link Tapo P110** is the best smart plug for most UK homes in 2025. It offers energy monitoring, reliable WiFi connectivity, a compact design, and excellent Home Assistant integration — all for around £15–16. It's the smart plug I recommend to beginners and experienced home automation enthusiasts alike.

## Do smart plugs work without the internet?

Most WiFi smart plugs require internet for initial setup and cloud-based control. However, **Zigbee smart plugs** like the IKEA TRÅDFRI outlet work fully locally. WiFi plugs like the Tapo P110 can also be controlled locally via Home Assistant once configured, bypassing the cloud entirely.

## Can smart plugs really save money on energy bills?

Yes — specifically smart plugs **with energy monitoring**. By identifying devices with high standby consumption or unexpected usage patterns, you can make informed decisions about usage habits. In my home, energy-monitoring smart plugs have identified hundreds of pounds of annual waste.

## Are smart plugs safe to leave on permanently?

Smart plugs from reputable brands (TP-Link, Meross, Amazon) are tested to UK/EU safety standards and safe to leave plugged in. Always check the plug's rated wattage limit (typically 2300W for UK sockets) and don't exceed it with high-draw appliances like electric heaters.

## What's the difference between WiFi and Zigbee smart plugs?

WiFi smart plugs connect directly to your home router — easy setup but adds to WiFi congestion and depends on cloud/internet. Zigbee plugs connect to a Zigbee coordinator and operate locally with lower latency and no cloud dependency. Zigbee plugs also extend the mesh network, which improves reliability for other Zigbee devices.
