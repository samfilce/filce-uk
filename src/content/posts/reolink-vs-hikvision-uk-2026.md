## Brand Background

### Reolink

Reolink was founded in 2009 and has rapidly grown into one of the most popular consumer security camera brands worldwide. They make cameras, NVRs, PoE switches, and solar-powered cameras aimed squarely at home users. Their products are available on Amazon UK and through their own website, with competitive pricing and free shipping.

What set Reolink apart was the decision to make their cameras genuinely locally controllable — RTSP streams, ONVIF support, and eventually a native Home Assistant integration. For the smart home community, this was a big deal.

### Hikvision

Hikvision is the world's largest manufacturer of video surveillance equipment by revenue. Founded in 2001 in Hangzhou, China, their cameras are used in professional installations globally — airports, retail, corporate security, and government buildings. Their home-oriented line (sold under the DS-2CD range) brings pro-grade build quality to the consumer market.

Hikvision cameras are renowned for their image processing, build quality, and long operational lifespan. They're harder to set up for beginners but trusted by professional installers who expect hardware to run for a decade without failure.

## Image Quality Comparison

### Resolution & Daylight Performance

Both brands offer 2MP, 4MP, and 4K (8MP) cameras. For new purchases in 2026, 4MP or 4K is the minimum worth considering — 1080p shows its age quickly when you need to zoom into footage.

Hikvision's image processing has a slight edge in daylight conditions. The colour science is more accurate, with less oversaturation in bright conditions and better handling of high-contrast scenes.

Reolink's image quality is genuinely competitive — particularly on their [Fisheye PoE](/posts/reolink-fisheye-camera-review/) and RLC-810A models. For most purposes, you won't notice the difference.

### Night Vision

This is where it gets interesting:

- **Hikvision IR night vision**: Produces cleaner, less noisy images in very low light. Their optical engineering is outstanding.
- **Reolink colour night vision**: Performs extremely well in real-world home conditions. UK streets have enough ambient light that colour footage is genuinely useful.

**Verdict:** Hikvision wins on pure technical image quality, especially in complete darkness. Reolink wins on colour night vision value for money.

## Home Assistant Integration

This is where Reolink has a clear advantage for smart home users.

### Reolink

Reolink has a **native Home Assistant integration**. It pulls in:
- Live camera streams
- Motion detection sensors
- Recording controls
- Person/vehicle/pet detection as binary sensors

Setup is genuinely straightforward: add the IP address, enter credentials, and you're done. All your cameras appear as entities without manual YAML configuration.

### Hikvision

Hikvision cameras work with Home Assistant via **ONVIF integration**. This requires more manual configuration:
- IP address
- ONVIF port
- Username/password
- Device profile selection

Once configured, streams and sensors work well. But there's no native Hikvision integration — you're reliant on generic protocols.

**Verdict:** Reolink is the clear winner for Home Assistant users.

## PoE & Power Requirements

Both brands use standard **802.3af PoE** (15.4W) for most cameras. This means:
- Any 802.3af PoE switch or NVR will power them
- No separate power supply needed
- Single Cat5e/Cat6 cable for data and power

Higher-end Hikvision cameras with PTZ motors or built-in heaters may need **802.3at PoE+** (30W). Reolink's home-focused range sticks to 802.3af across their product line.

## Build Quality & Longevity

### Reolink

Build quality is **good for the price**. The housings are mostly plastic, but weatherproofing is solid (IP66 rated on outdoor models). After two years of outdoor use, my Reolink cameras show no degradation.

The weak point tends to be long-term firmware updates — while Reolink does update their cameras, the pace is slower than Hikvision.

### Hikvision

Build quality is **pro-grade**. Metal housings, better gaskets, and a reputation for running 5–10 years without failure. This is why professional installers specify Hikvision — they don't want callbacks.

Firmware support is excellent, with regular security patches and feature updates.

**Verdict:** Hikvision wins on longevity and build quality. Reolink is perfectly adequate for home use.

## FAQ

### Is Reolink worth it for UK buyers?
Yes. Reolink offers excellent value for money, solid Home Assistant compatibility, and decent build quality. For most home users, Reolink cameras outperform cameras costing twice as much from lesser-known brands. We've tested the Fisheye PoE extensively — see our [full review](/posts/reolink-fisheye-camera-review/).

### Is Hikvision banned in the UK?
No. While Hikvision has faced scrutiny due to Chinese state ownership, there is no UK-wide ban on Hikvision cameras for home use. Government and sensitive installations have different restrictions. For typical home CCTV use, Hikvision cameras are legal and widely available from UK retailers.

### Can I mix Reolink and Hikvision cameras on the same NVR?
Generally no — NVRs from either brand tend to be proprietary. However, both brands support ONVIF, so you can use a third-party NVR or software (like Frigate, Shinobi, or Blue Iris) to record from mixed brands.

### Which has better Home Assistant support?
Reolink has a native Home Assistant integration with automatic discovery. Hikvision works via ONVIF, which requires manual configuration. Reolink is the clear winner for HA users.

### Do I need an NVR for PoE cameras?
No. You can connect PoE cameras to a PoE switch and stream to a home server running Frigate, Shinobi, or similar. Many Home Assistant users skip the NVR entirely. See our guide on [the best NVRs for home CCTV](/posts/best-nvr-home-cctv-uk-2026/) if you prefer a dedicated recorder.

### Will Reolink cameras work with Hikvision NVR (or vice versa)?
Sometimes, via ONVIF — but compatibility isn't guaranteed. If you want guaranteed compatibility, stick to one brand for cameras and NVR, or use a third-party recording solution.

### What's the best PoE switch for home CCTV?
For home setups, an 8-port or 16-port 802.3af PoE switch is ideal. Budget options like YuanLey work fine. We reviewed our [18-port YuanLey PoE switch](/posts/yuanley-18-port-poe-switch-review/) alongside our camera setup.
