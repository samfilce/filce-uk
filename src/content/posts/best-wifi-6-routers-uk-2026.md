## Why WiFi 6 Matters in 2026

If you've got ultrafast broadband (300Mbps+), your ISP router is likely the bottleneck. Here's what WiFi 6 actually changes:

**Higher throughput** — WiFi 6 supports speeds up to 9.6Gbps (compared to 3.5Gbps on WiFi 5). Your gigabit broadband can actually reach your devices.

**Better device handling** — OFDMA and MU-MIMO mean your router can talk to multiple devices simultaneously without congestion. In a household with 20+ devices, this is the difference between smooth streaming and constant buffering.

**Lower latency** — Target Wake Time (TWT) reduces contention, which means better responsiveness for gaming and video calls.

**Improved range** — 1024-QAM encoding squeezes more data into each transmission, and beamforming is more refined.

If you're on standard broadband (50-100Mbps), a WiFi 6 router still helps — just less dramatically. If you're on 500Mbps+ fibre, the difference is night and day.

## How to Choose the Right Router

Ask yourself these three questions:

1. **What's your broadband speed?** If you're on 500Mbps+, get a router with 1Gbps+ ports. If you're on 100Mbps, any WiFi 6 router will feel fast.

2. **Do you have dead zones?** If yes, get a mesh system (Deco XE75, Eero Pro 6E). If coverage is fine, a single router saves money.

3. **How many devices?** Under 20 devices: RT-AX59U. 20-50: RT-BE88U or Nighthawk AX12. 50+: Nighthawk AX12 with its 12-stream capacity.

## FAQ

### What's the difference between WiFi 6 and WiFi 6E?

WiFi 6E adds the 6GHz frequency band, which is much less congested than the 2.4GHz and 5GHz bands used by WiFi 6. If you live in a densely populated area (flat, terraced house), 6E can make a noticeable difference. If you're in a detached house, standard WiFi 6 is probably fine.

### Do I need WiFi 7 router?

Not yet. WiFi 7 (BE) routers are available but the devices that support it are limited. A WiFi 6 or 6E router will serve you well for 3-5 years. Consider WiFi 7 if you're building new and want maximum future-proofing.

### Will a WiFi 6 router speed up my internet?

Only if your current router is the bottleneck. If you're on 100Mbps broadband with a 5-year-old router, upgrading to WiFi 6 will likely give you the full 100Mbps. If you're already getting your full speed, the upgrade brings better range and device handling, not faster raw internet.

### Can I use a mesh system with my existing router?

Yes — most mesh systems can run in access point mode, where they use your existing router for the internet connection and handle WiFi coverage themselves. This avoids double-NAT issues.
